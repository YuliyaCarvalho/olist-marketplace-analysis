# Olist Dashboard — Marketplace Overview 

**Business Context:** *Core Question: What’s happening overall?* 
This page serves as the entry point to the Olist dashboard. It provides a high-level summary of marketplace size, commercial performance, customer reach, seller participation, customer satisfaction, and top-level growth patterns over time.

## 1. Report page purpose

- **Page Role:** Executive overview
- **Primary Objective:** Summarize the marketplace’s overall commercial performance, customer base, fulfillment quality, and high-level revenue composition.
- **Grain of Analysis:** Primarily order-level, with supporting customer-, seller-, region-, and product-category-level aggregations

**Key Business Questions:**

* How large is the marketplace in terms of billed value, GMV, orders, customers, and sellers?  
* How has marketplace performance evolved over time?  
* Is growth driven by both order volume and GMV, or are these diverging?  
* Which customer regions and product categories contribute the most to GMV?  
* Is operational performance strong enough to support healthy marketplace growth?

**Page-Level Notes:**

➡︎ This page is designed for high-level monitoring, rather than deep diagnosis  
➡︎ This page combines broad platform-level KPIs with the dashboard’s cleaned analytical metrics  
➡︎ Metrics based on the core valid-order cohort are explicitly distinguished from broader marketplace measures

---

## 2. Slicers

### 2.1 Reporting Period

- **Field Used:** `dim_date[YearMonth Label]`
- **Purpose:** Filters the dashboard to a selected time window (YearMonth-basis)

### 2.2 Customer State

- **Field Used:** `customers_final[customer_state]`
- **Purpose:** Filters results by customer's location state

### 2.3 Customer Region

- **Field Used:** `customers_final[Customer Region]`
- **Purpose:** Filters results by broader customer geography

### 2.4 Product Category

- **Field Used:** `order_items_final[product_category]`
- **Purpose:** Filters the page by product category based on categories present in actual ordered items

**Slicer Notes:**

➡︎ This slicer uses `order_items_final` rather than `products_final`  
➡︎ The transactional order-items table keeps the slicer tied to categories that were **actually involved** in marketplace orders  
➡︎ This avoids using the broader product catalog table, which represents all listed products in the dataset, rather than only products observed in order activity  
➡︎ The `Net GMV` contribution chart by product category is intentionally not affected by this slicer, so that category shares remain stable and comparable

## 3. KPI cards

### 3.1 Total Billed

- **Display Name:** Total Billed
- **Type:** Card
- **Measure:** [Total Billed](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#total-billed)
- **Used As:** Marketplace-wide gross billed amount headline metric

**Business Definition:**  
Represents the total monetary value of all recorded payments, calculated as the sum of `payment_value` across all transactions.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ This card should be clearly distinguished from `Net GMV`  

---

### 3.2 Net GMV

- **Display Name:** Net GMV
- **Type:** Card
- **Measure:** [Net GMV](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#net-gmv)

**Business Definition:**  
Shows the total GMV generated by the dashboard’s valid delivered order cohort.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ This is the main cleaned revenue metric used across the dashboard  
➡︎ It is more analytically reliable than broader billed-value measures, because it applies cohort-quality filters  
➡︎ This card acts as a core benchmark for many other visuals

---

### 3.3 Order Volume

- **Display Name:** Order Volume
- **Type:** Card
- **Measure:** [Order Volume](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#order-volume)

**Business Definition:**  
  Counts distinct marketplace orders in the selected context, across all order statuses.

---

### 3.4 Total Customers

- **Display Name:** Total Customers
- **Type:** Card
- **Measure:** [Total Customers](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#total-customers)

**Business Definition:**  
Counts distinct customers in the selected context.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ This is a broad platform-level customer count metric  
➡︎ It is not restricted to customers with valid orders  
➡︎ This card helps contextualize marketplace reach and customer base size

---

### 3.5 AOV

- **Display Name:** AOV
- **Type:** Card
- **Measure:** [AOV](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#aov)

**Business Definition:**  
Shows average order value for the selected context.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Calculated as `Net GMV / Valid Delivered Orders`  
➡︎ Helps distinguish growth driven by higher order volume from growth driven by higher order value  
➡︎ Because `Net GMV` is based on `payment_value`, this metric reflects billed order value rather than item price alone  
➡︎ Should be interpreted together with order volume trends

---

### 3.6 Order Fulfillment

- **Display Name:** Order Fulfillment
- **Type:** Card
- **Measure:** [Fulfillment Rate](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#fulfillment-rate)

**Business Definition:**  
Shows the share of total orders that were successfully delivered and included in the dashboard’s core valid-order cohort.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Calculated as `Valid Delivered Orders / Order Volume`  
➡︎ Combines a broad platform denominator with a stricter **delivered-and-valid** numerator  
➡︎ Provides a high-level view of how much of total marketplace order volume converts into clean fulfilled orders  
➡︎ Should be interpreted as a dashboard cohort conversion metric, not as a narrow logistics KPI

---

### 3.7 Total Sellers

- **Display Name:** Total Sellers
- **Type:** Card
- **Measure:** [Total Sellers](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#total-sellers)

**Business Definition:**  
Shows the number of distinct sellers in the selected context.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Counts **distinct seller** records, rather than only sellers with confirmed transactional activity  
➡︎ Provides a high-level view of marketplace supply-side size  
➡︎ Should be interpreted as a broad **seller base metric**, rather than a strict seller activity KPI

---

### 3.8 Avg Customer Rating

- **Display Name:** Avg Customer Rating
- **Type:** Gauge / KPI
- **Measure:** [Average Review Score](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#avg-review-score)

**Business Definition:**  
Shows the average customer review score across available review records in the selected context.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Calculated as the average of non-blank `review_stars` values  
➡︎ Provides a high-level view of customer satisfaction  
➡︎ Is based on review records and is not limited to delivered orders  
➡︎ Should be interpreted as a broad sentiment metric, rather than a cleaned valid-order KPI

## 4. Charts and Visuals

### 4.1 Marketplace Growth: Net GMV and Order Volume

- **Visual Type:** Dual-axis line chart
- **X-Axis:** `dim_date[Month Start]`
- **Y-Axis (Left):** [Net GMV](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#net-gmv)
- **Y-Axis (Right):** [Order Volume](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#order-volume)
- **Data Labels:** Selected data points only

**Business Definition:**  
Shows how marketplace value and order activity evolved over time by comparing monthly **Net GMV** and **Order Volume** on the same time axis.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Plots monthly `Net GMV` and `Order Volume` together to show overall marketplace growth  
➡︎ Uses `Month Start` as the time axis, aggregating results at monthly level  
➡︎ Combines a filtered revenue metric with a broader platform order-count metric  
➡︎ Selected data labels are shown only on key points to improve readability and avoid clutter  
➡︎ September and October 2018 are excluded from presentation because the dataset tail is inconsistent for trend analysis, even though the platform itself remained operational  
➡︎ The public Olist dataset is generally described as covering orders from 2016 to 2018, but end-of-period records are affected by snapshot timing and downstream event lag, so this page presents the more stable reporting window through August 2018.

**How to Read This Visual:**

➡︎ The red line shows the monthly trend in `Net GMV`  
➡︎ The blue line shows the monthly trend in `Order Volume`  
➡︎ Similar movement in both lines suggests that revenue growth is broadly supported by order growth  
➡︎ Divergence between the two lines may indicate changes in average order value or customer mix (displayed in tooltips), or order composition

**Interpretation Notes:**

* This is the main top-line growth visual on the page  
* `Net GMV` reflects the dashboard’s cleaned valid delivered order cohort, while `Order Volume` is a broader marketplace count metric  
* The chart is intended to show directional growth patterns, rather than exact one-to-one comparability between the two lines

---

### 4.2 GMV & Orders - Monthly Momentum (%)

- **Visual Type:** Column and line combo chart
- **X-Axis:** `dim_date[Month Start]`
- **Column Y-Axis:** [GMV MoM %](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#gmv-mom-percent)
- **Line Y-Axis:** [Orders MoM %](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#orders-mom-percent)
- **Data Labels:** Selected data points only


**Business Definition:**  
Shows month-over-month growth rates for marketplace value and order activity, highlighting periods of acceleration, slowdown, and contraction.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Plots monthly percentage change in `Net GMV` and `Order Volume`, rather than their absolute values  
➡︎ Uses `Month Start` as the time axis, aggregating growth at monthly level  
➡︎ The columns show `GMV MoM %`, while the line shows `Orders MoM %`  
➡︎ Selected data labels are shown only on key points to improve readability and avoid clutter  
➡︎ For reporting consistency, the visual is limited to February 2017 through August 2018  
➡︎ September to December 2016 and January 2017 are excluded because very low or irregular starting volumes would distort month-over-month comparisons  
➡︎ September and October 2018 are excluded because the dataset tail is not stable enough for clean trend reporting

**How to Read This Visual:**

➡︎ Values above zero indicate month-over-month growth  
➡︎ Values below zero indicate month-over-month decline  
➡︎ Larger positive spikes indicate faster expansion relative to the previous month  
➡︎ Divergence between GMV momentum and order momentum may indicate changes in average order value, product mix, or customer behavior

**Interpretation Notes:**

* This chart emphasizes growth speed and volatility rather than marketplace size  
* `GMV MoM %` is based on the filtered `Net GMV` metric, while `Orders MoM %` is based on the broader `Order Volume` metric  
* The visual uses a restricted reporting window to avoid distorted MoM results caused by sparse early months and unstable end-of-period data  
* This visual is most useful for spotting directional shifts and inflection points, not for judging long-run performance in isolation

### 4.3 Customer Base Growth

- **Visual Type:** Area chart
- **X-Axis:** `dim_date[Month Start]`
- **Y-Axis:** [Total Customers](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#total-customers)
- **Data Labels:** Selected data points only

**Business Definition:**  
Shows how the marketplace customer base evolves over time by plotting distinct customers at monthly level.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Uses `Month Start` as the time axis, aggregating results at monthly level  
➡︎ Plots `Total Customers` to show how customer reach changes over time  
➡︎ Selected data labels are shown only on key points to improve readability and avoid clutter  
➡︎ The visual ends in August 2018, with September and October 2018 excluded from presentation because the dataset tail is not stable enough for clean trend reporting  
➡︎ This visual is intended to show broad customer base expansion rather than a cleaned valid-order customer cohort

**How to Read This Visual:**

➡︎ Rising values indicate growth in the customer base over time  
➡︎ Plateaus suggest slower customer expansion or stabilization  
➡︎ Sudden jumps may reflect periods of stronger acquisition or seasonal marketplace activity  
➡︎ Later declines or flatter periods can indicate slower growth, rather than necessarily customer loss

**Interpretation Notes:**

* This is a broad platform-level customer trend visual  
* It uses `Total Customers`, which is wider than the dashboard’s valid-order cohort    
* The chart is most useful for understanding marketplace reach and growth trajectory at a high level  
* It should be interpreted alongside order and GMV trends, rather than as a standalone retention metric

### 4.4 Net GMV by Customer Region

- **Visual Type:** Horizontal bar chart
- **Y-Axis:** [Customer Region](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#customer-region)
- **X-Axis:** [Net GMV](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#net-gmv)
- **Tooltip:** [Customer Region GMV %](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#customer-region-gmv-percent)
- **Visual-Level Filters:** None

**Business Definition:**  
Shows how the dashboard’s cleaned GMV is distributed across customer regions, highlighting the relative contribution of each geographic market.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Uses the calculated column `Customer Region` to group customers into five broader Brazilian regions  
➡︎ Plots `Net GMV` by region to compare regional demand contribution  
➡︎ Data values displayed are `Customer Region GMV %`, showing each region’s share of total `Net GMV` in the current page context  
➡︎ No visual-level filters are applied, so the chart reflects the full page context  
➡︎ The chart is sorted in descending order of `Net GMV`

**How to Read This Visual:**

➡︎ Longer bars indicate higher regional contribution to `Net GMV`  
➡︎ Percentage labels or tooltip values show each region’s share of the total regional GMV shown on the page  
➡︎ The chart highlights whether marketplace demand is broadly distributed or concentrated in a small number of regions

**Interpretation Notes:**

* This is a demand-side geography visual, based on customer location rather than seller location  
* It uses the cleaned `Net GMV` metric, so values reflect the dashboard’s valid delivered order cohort  
* Regional grouping improves readability and supports executive comparison, but it compresses state-level variation within each region

### 4.5 Net GMV Contribution by Product Category

- **Visual Type:** Horizontal bar chart
- **Y-Axis:** `products_final[product_category_eng]`
- **X-Axis:** [Net GMV](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#net-gmv)
- **Tooltip:** [Category GMV %](./olist-dashboard-measures-and-calculated-columns.md#category-gmv-percent)
- **Visual-Level Filters:** None
- **Interaction:** Responds to page slicers (except `Product Category`)

**Business Definition:**  
Shows how the dashboard’s cleaned GMV is distributed across product categories, highlighting which categories contribute the most to marketplace value.

**Documentation Notes:**

➡︎ Uses `products_final[product_category_eng]` to group orders by product category  
➡︎ Plots `Net GMV` by category to compare category-level revenue contribution  
➡︎ Tooltip includes `Category GMV %`, showing each category’s share of total `Net GMV` with `products_final` filters removed in the denominator  
➡︎ The visual is scrollable and includes the full set of product categories rather than only a fixed top-N subset  
➡︎ The chart is sorted in descending order of `Net GMV`  
➡︎ To preserve a consistent category contribution view, this visual is not affected by the `Product Category` slicer

**How to Read This Visual:**

➡︎ Longer bars indicate higher category contribution to `Net GMV`  
➡︎ Tooltip percentages show each category’s share of total marketplace `Net GMV` in the current non-product-filtered context  
➡︎ The chart helps identify whether marketplace revenue is diversified across categories or concentrated in a smaller number of high-contributing segments

**Interpretation Notes:**

* This is a stable product-mix contribution visual based on the cleaned `Net GMV` metric  
* It remains responsive to broader page context such as reporting period and customer geography  
* It is intentionally isolated from the `Product Category` slicer so that category shares remain comparable and conceptually consistent  
* The chart is best used to understand category concentration, not to analyze a user-selected subset of categories

## 5. Page summary

**Main Insights:**

➡︎ The page establishes overall marketplace scale through GMV, orders, customers, sellers, etc.  
➡︎ Growth is shown through both absolute trend and month-over-month momentum, giving a view of both: scale and speed  
➡︎ Regional and category visuals show that marketplace value is not evenly distributed across demand segments  
➡︎ Operational and customer-quality KPIs help balance commercial growth with service performance

**Why This Overview Page Matters:**

➡︎ It gives decision-makers a fast view of marketplace health  
➡︎ It anchors the rest of the dashboard by introducing the core KPIs used in deeper pages  
➡︎ It frames later analysis of customer behavior, operations, logistics, and customer experience
